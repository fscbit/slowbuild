The Timeless Art of Tarot

Tarot is one of the world's most beloved divination systems, with roots tracing back to 15th-century Europe. What began as playing cards evolved into a profound tool for introspection, guidance, and spiritual exploration. The 78-card deck — divided into the Major Arcana (archetypal life themes) and Minor Arcana (everyday situations) — serves as a mirror to the soul, reflecting our deepest questions and illuminating possible paths forward.

Each tarot card carries rich symbolism layered with centuries of interpretation. The Fool's leap of faith, the High Priestess's intuition, the Tower's sudden transformation — these archetypes speak a universal language that transcends culture and time. When you draw cards with intention, you tap into a dialogue between your conscious mind and the wisdom of the collective unconscious.

How Our 3-Card Spread Works

We offer the classic 3-card spread, one of the most accessible yet powerful tarot layouts. The three positions represent:

  • Card 1 — The Past: Events, influences, or patterns that have shaped your current situation. Understanding where you've been is essential to knowing where you're going.
  • Card 2 — The Present: Your current circumstances, challenges, or state of mind. This is the heart of the reading, showing what's happening right now.
  • Card 3 — The Future: Potential outcomes and directions. Remember that the future is not fixed — tarot shows possibilities, not certainties.

Our AI-powered interpretation combines traditional tarot meanings with nuanced, context-aware insights. Each card is explained in detail, and the relationships between the three cards are woven into a coherent narrative that speaks directly to your situation.

How to Get the Most From Your Reading

Before you draw your cards, take a moment to center yourself. Breathe deeply three times. Form a clear, open-ended question in your mind — not "Will I get that job?" but rather "What should I understand about my career path?" Focus on your question as you use the tool below.

When your cards appear, read the interpretation with an open mind. Sometimes the most powerful insights come from cards that initially seem unrelated. Trust that whatever appears has meaning for you. Tarot is a conversation — the cards speak, and your intuition listens.

Major Arcana Highlights

The 22 Major Arcana cards represent the soul's journey — the Fool's path from innocence to enlightenment. Cards like The Sun (joy and vitality), The Moon (intuition and illusion), and Death (transformation and rebirth) are powerful signposts on this journey. When Major Arcana cards appear in your spread, pay special attention — they signal life themes that carry deep significance.

The Minor Arcana's four suits — Wands (passion and creativity), Cups (emotions and relationships), Swords (intellect and conflict), and Pentacles (material world and abundance) — fill in the practical details of daily life. Together, the Major and Minor Arcana create a complete picture that speaks to both your spiritual journey and your everyday reality.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a 3-card tarot spread?

A 3-card tarot spread is a classic layout representing Past, Present, and Future. The first card reveals past influences, the second shows your current situation, and the third indicates future possibilities. It's the most popular spread for quick, comprehensive readings.

Can I get an accurate tarot reading online for free?

Yes! Online tarot readings work through the principle of synchronicity — the cards you draw reflect meaningful patterns in your life. Our AI-powered system provides accurate interpretations based on traditional tarot meanings combined with modern psychological insights.

How often should I get a tarot reading?

There's no strict rule, but many people find weekly or monthly readings most helpful. Avoid asking the same question repeatedly — give situations time to develop. Tarot works best as a tool for reflection and gaining new perspectives.
