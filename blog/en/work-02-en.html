Lost Your ID, Keys, or USB Drive? Xiao Liu Ren Lost-Item Method — A Worker's Survival Guide

June 21, 2026 · 7 min read

It's 7:30 AM. Your alarm has gone off three times. You spring out of bed, wash up, get dressed in one fluid motion. One last thing before heading out—grab your keys. Keys? Not on the shoe cabinet. Not in your pocket. Not in yesterday's bag. You tear apart your rental apartment. Minutes tick by. In your head, you can already hear your boss: "How many times this month?"

Losing things—every working person knows this pain. ID cards, social security cards, work badges, USB drives (with tonight's deadline report on them), umbrellas, transit cards. Every lost item costs you a day of peace of mind plus a few hundred in replacement fees. The worst part isn't even the loss itself—it's the certainty that you absolutely remember putting it right there, and it simply isn't.

Today I'm teaching you an ancient technique: Xiao Liu Ren Lost-Item Method. No birth chart needed. No props. Just your fingers. And yes—it actually works.

What Is Xiao Liu Ren? The Ancient "GPS" for Lost Items

Xiao Liu Ren, also known as "Ma Qian Ke" or "Zhuge Ma Qian Ke," is a quick divination method legendarily invented by Zhuge Liang. Simply put, it's a rapid prediction tool that uses three numbers—lunar month, lunar day, and time period—computed on your palm. Unlike I-Ching (Liu Yao)—which requires coin tossing, hexagram construction, and interpretation—Xiao Liu Ren takes under a minute, right on your own hand.

The six palm positions are called: Da An (Great Peace), Liu Lian (Lingering), Su Xi (Swift Joy), Chi Kou (Red Mouth), Xiao Ji (Minor Luck), and Kong Wang (Empty Void). Each position has a corresponding meaning, and for finding lost items, the ancients already mapped it all out clearly.

How to Use Xiao Liu Ren to Find Things: Step-by-Step Tutorial

Step 1: Get Your Three Numbers

You need three numbers: current lunar month, current lunar day, and the current time period (Shi Chen).

Lunar month: Month 1 is 1, month 2 is 2, etc. If you don't know today's lunar date, just glance at your phone calendar. Time period conversion:

🕐 Time Period Chart (Worker-Friendly Edition)

  • Zi (23:00-1:00) = 1
  • Chou (1:00-3:00) = 2
  • Yin (3:00-5:00) = 3
  • Mao (5:00-7:00) = 4
  • Chen (7:00-9:00) = 5
  • Si (9:00-11:00) = 6
  • Wu (11:00-13:00) = 7
  • Wei (13:00-15:00) = 8
  • Shen (15:00-17:00) = 9
  • You (17:00-19:00) = 10
  • Xu (19:00-21:00) = 11
  • Hai (21:00-23:00) = 12

Example: June 21, 2026, 8 AM, you discover something's missing. Lunar: Month 5, Day 6. Time: Chen (7-9 AM) = 5. Your three numbers: 5, 6, 5.

Step 2: Calculate on Your Palm

Hold out your left hand (either hand works, use what's comfortable). Starting from the base of the index finger, the six positions are distributed clockwise:

✋ Palm Positions (on left index to pinky fingers)

  • 1. Da An — Index finger base (near thumb web)
  • 2. Liu Lian — Index fingertip
  • 3. Su Xi — Middle fingertip
  • 4. Chi Kou — Ring fingertip
  • 5. Xiao Ji — Ring finger base
  • 6. Kong Wang — Middle finger base

Calculation steps:

  1. Start from Da An (1), count clockwise to the lunar month. Month 5: Da An(1)→Liu Lian(2)→Su Xi(3)→Chi Kou(4)→Xiao Ji(5).
  2. From Xiao Ji, count to the lunar day. Day 6: Xiao Ji(1)→Kong Wang(2)→Da An(3)→Liu Lian(4)→Su Xi(5)→Chi Kou(6).
  3. From Chi Kou, count to the time period. Chen=5: Chi Kou(1)→Xiao Ji(2)→Kong Wang(3)→Da An(4)→Liu Lian(5).

Final position: Liu Lian. Now for the interpretation.

Lost Item Meanings for the Six Palm Positions: Where Is It?

🔍 Lost Item Quick Reference

  • Da An: It's nearby! Still at home or near the office, east direction. Will turn up on its own soon. Don't panic.
  • Liu Lian: Something is covering it, or it's wedged in a crack. Look south. Thoroughly search piles of stuff.
  • Su Xi: Not lost, but someone moved it. Ask people around you. Might be in a common area.
  • Chi Kou: Not optimistic. Possibly lost outside or taken by someone. Check west, but prepare mentally that it may be gone.
  • Xiao Ji: Best outcome! Someone found it or placed it safely. Look northeast—someone may already be trying to return it to you.
  • Kong Wang: Worst result. Truly lost or destroyed. Check north one last time, then let it go. Don't waste more time.
Real case: A friend of mine, after moving last year, couldn't find her ID card anywhere. Xiao Liu Ren landed on Xiao Ji, suggesting northeast. She remembered putting a document folder on the northeast corner of her new bookshelf on moving day—and there it was. She'd completely forgotten.

Advanced: I-Ching (Liu Yao) for Lost Items — When Xiao Liu Ren Isn't Enough

Xiao Liu Ren is great for "quick location." But if you need more detail—whether it was stolen or just misplaced, when you'll get it back, what to do if it's gone—then it's time to bring out I-Ching (Liu Yao).

Liu Yao uses three coins (regular coins work fine), tossed six times to form a hexagram. Can't read hexagrams? No problem—SlowBuild generates and interprets automatically. But know this core logic: for lost items, watch the "Wealth Line" and "Officer Line." Plain language: the Wealth Line represents your item (it's your property), the Officer Line represents "who or what took it." If the Wealth Line is strong and unclashed, the item is definitely still around and you'll find it. If the Wealth Line is being thoroughly suppressed by the Officer Line, it may truly be gone.

Frequently Losing Things? Your Luck Might Be Warning You

If you're not losing one thing but everything—keys, phone, documents, even your train of thought—on a regular basis, it might not just be carelessness.

In destiny analysis, frequent lost items often relate to a damaged "Resource Star." The Resource Star represents protection, stability, memory. When the annual cycle clashes with your Resource Star, people become forgetful, scattered, and absent-minded. You're not getting old—this year's energy field has bugged your "storage system."

Additionally, losing small items can be a form of "wealth leakage." If lately you're not just losing things but also bleeding money unexpectedly (cracked phone screen, fines, deductions), you might be entering a period of financial turbulence. How to fix it? That's for the next article.

Worker's Anti-Loss Protocol: Science + Metaphysics

  • Photo backup important items. Front and back of your ID, social security number, critical USB contents—back them up before you lose them.
  • Fixed spots for fixed items. Keys always on the hook by the door. USB always in the first pocket of your bag. Build the "return-to-place" habit—it beats any divination.
  • Don't panic when you lose something. Do Xiao Liu Ren first. Once you have a clue, then search. When you're panicked, your vision narrows—you can miss things right in front of you.
  • Liu Lian result? Focus on crevices. Sofa cracks, bed crevices, drawer gaps, under stacks of papers—Liu Lian items are almost always wedged in these places.

One last thing: losing stuff is annoying, but don't let it ruin your whole day. Anything replaceable with money isn't a big deal. The truly irreplaceable things... well, check Xiao Liu Ren to see if they're worth the search.
