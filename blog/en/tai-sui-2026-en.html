2026 Tai Sui Survival Guide: Complete Remedies for Affected Zodiac Signs

June 21, 2026 · Tai Sui Remedies

What Is Tai Sui?

Tai Sui, also known as the "Grand Duke Jupiter" or "Year God," is the deity presiding over each year in the Chinese traditional calendar. Every year has a different Tai Sui deity on duty, governing the fortunes and misfortunes of that year. "Offending Tai Sui" means your zodiac sign has an unfavorable relationship — such as clashing, harming, breaking, or punishing — with the Tai Sui of that year. 2026 is the Bing Wu (Fire Horse) year, and Tai Sui resides in the south.

The Four Types of Tai Sui Offenses in 2026

1. Zodiac Year (Zhi Tai Sui) — Horse Most Severe

Definition: Your zodiac year (Ben Ming Nian). Horse-born people face Zhi Tai Sui in 2026.

Impact: The most volatile energy. Everything is prone to disruption — career instability, financial ups and downs, health concerns. But it can also swing toward major breakthroughs (polarization).

High-risk months: Lunar Month 5 (Wu month, June–July) and Lunar Month 11 (Zi month, December–January).

2. Clashing Tai Sui (Chong Tai Sui) — Rat Severe

Definition: The Rat (Zi) and Horse (Wu) are direct opposites. Rat-born clash with Tai Sui in 2026.

Impact: "Clashing means movement." Expect significant changes — job changes, relocation, reshuffled relationships. Not all changes are bad, but the ride will be bumpy.

High-risk months: Lunar Months 5 and 11 (Zi-Wu clash months).

3. Harming Tai Sui (Hai Tai Sui) — Ox Moderate

Definition: The Ox (Chou) and Horse (Wu) harm each other. Ox-born harm Tai Sui in 2026.

Impact: Mainly affects interpersonal relationships. You may face scheming from petty people, misunderstandings in teams, or workplace ostracism. Harming Tai Sui isn't as intense as clashing, but it lasts longer and casts a wider net.

4. Breaking Tai Sui (Po Tai Sui) — Rabbit Moderate

Definition: The Rabbit (Mao) and Horse (Wu) break each other. Rabbit-born break Tai Sui in 2026.

Impact: Plans get disrupted. Even well-prepared things encounter surprises. Documents and contracts are prone to errors. In a breaking year, always have a Plan B.

12 Tai Sui Remedies

  1. Pray to Tai Sui: Visit a temple early in the year — ideally before the 15th of the first lunar month — to pay respects. This is the most traditional and important remedy.
  2. Obtain a Tai Sui Amulet: Get one from a proper Daoist temple and carry it with you. Burn it on the 24th day of the 12th lunar month as a closing ritual.
  3. Wear red: Red underwear, a red string bracelet, or a red belt strengthens your personal energy field during your zodiac year.
  4. Wear harmonious zodiac charms: Horse-born can wear Tiger or Dog (three-harmony combo); Rat-born can wear Ox or Dragon; Ox-born can wear Rat or Snake; Rabbit-born can wear Dog or Pig.
  5. Do good deeds: Donate, give blood, help the elderly and children. Positive karma neutralizes negative factors.
  6. Avoid funerals: Your energy field is weaker in a Tai Sui year. Skip funerals and visits to the seriously ill.
  7. Don't argue: Stay calm and let things go. Small verbal spats can escalate quickly during Tai Sui years.
  8. Avoid major life moves: Postpone marriage, moving, business openings, and renovations if possible. If unavoidable, pick an auspicious date.
  9. Adjust home feng shui: Keep the south (Tai Sui direction) clean. No renovation, hammering, or heavy objects there.
  10. Get a checkup: Do a health screening early in the year. Forewarned is forearmed.
  11. Mindset adjustment: Offending Tai Sui doesn't mean "you're doomed" — it means "be more careful." The right mindset is the best amulet.
  12. Harness Fire energy: 2026 is a Fire year. Wear more red, light candles, use warm-toned lighting — amplify the positive side of Fire.

FAQ

Q: If my sign doesn't offend Tai Sui, am I in the clear?
Not entirely. You just don't have a direct bad relationship with the year god. Your actual fortune depends on your full BaZi and current Luck Cycle. But non-offending signs do tend to have a smoother ride.

Q: Can I get married during a Tai Sui year?
Traditionally, marrying in your zodiac year is discouraged but not absolutely forbidden. If the date is already set, pick an auspicious day and carry a Tai Sui amulet. For those merely clashing, harming, or breaking Tai Sui, marriage is less of a concern.

Q: What jewelry should I wear during a Tai Sui year?
Red items (red string, red agate), zodiac charms compatible with your sign, black obsidian (for protection), or golden rutilated quartz (for wealth and energy clearing) are all good choices.
