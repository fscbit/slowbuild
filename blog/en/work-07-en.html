Feel Like You Have Bad Luck? BaZi Special Charts Reveal How Ordinary People Can Turn It Around

June 21, 2026 · 7 min read

Late at night, scrolling social media—your college roommate bought a house, your former coworker just got promoted to director, your high school friend is vacationing with their kids in the Maldives. You put down your phone, stare at the ceiling, and that voice comes again: "Why does everyone else have it so good while I'm such a failure? Is my fate just... this?"

If you often feel this way—can't do anything right, luck always worse than others, hard work gets you nowhere—this article is written specifically for you.

Let me start with something that might shatter your understanding: the "best" BaZi charts are often not the "balanced perfect" ones—they're the ones that are extremely biased.

What Is a "Good Chart"? You Might Have BaZi All Wrong

The layperson's understanding: the more balanced the Five Elements, the better. Missing Water? Add Water. Missing Metal? Add Metal. This is the "Balancing Chart" (Fu Yi Ge) mindset. And yes, most people's charts follow this balancing approach.

But destiny analysis recognizes several "Special Chart Structures" that completely disregard the "balance" playbook. Their defining characteristic is "extreme bias"—one element so overwhelmingly dominant it becomes the entire chart's "king." To a layperson, these charts look terrible: "This broken thing is missing so many elements—how could it be any good?" Yet precisely these extremely biased charts, when they hit the right Luck Cycle, can achieve extraordinary things.

Destiny Iron Law: Extremely strong charts and extremely weak charts often have far more explosive power than "half-full" balanced charts. The key is—you need to know your chart structure, then walk the right Luck Cycles.

Three Most Common Special Charts: See If You Have One

1. Follow-the-Strong Chart (Cong Qiang / Cong Wang): You're Not Weak—You're Overwhelmingly Strong

If one element absolutely dominates your BaZi—say almost everything is Wood and Water, with virtually no Metal or Earth—you may have a "Follow-the-Strong" chart.

People with Follow-the-Strong charts share one trait: they look "abnormal." While others follow the rules, they're off doing their own thing. When others write them off, they somehow succeed anyway. Steve Jobs had a classic Follow-the-Strong chart—almost entirely Metal and Earth, extremely biased. But when he walked his Luck Cycle, he changed the world.

The key for Follow-the-Strong: you must walk a Luck Cycle that "goes with the flow." Whatever is dominant in your chart, the Luck Cycle must continue strengthening it. If instead you hit a cycle that suppresses your dominant element, you'll suffer enormously, feeling blocked at every turn.

If you have a Follow-the-Strong chart but things have always gone poorly—it's not that your fate is bad, it's that you haven't hit your Luck Cycle yet.

2. Follow-the-Weak Chart (Cong Ruo / Cong Sha / Cong Cai): You Thought You Were Weak—Actually You're a Wolf in Sheep's Clothing

Opposite of Follow-the-Strong: in a Follow-the-Weak chart, the Day Master is extremely weak while the forces controlling you (Officer/7 Killings) or draining you (Wealth Stars) are overwhelmingly strong. The Day Master has completely "surrendered"—stopped resisting—and instead rides the wave of the stronger forces.

From the outside, Follow-the-Weak people look "spineless," "go-with-the-flow," "wishy-washy." But in destiny terms, when they walk a "go-with-the-flow" Luck Cycle that continues strengthening the forces controlling them, they often soar on others' momentum.

Follow-the-Weak workers share one trait: they thrive inside powerful organizations or platforms. Try to solo in a small company? Total flop. Join a big company? Suddenly unstoppable. Because their destiny is to "follow," not to "carry."

Plain language: If you have a Follow-the-Weak chart, stop dreaming about entrepreneurship, solo ventures, carrying everything yourself. Find a big platform, a big backer. Follow the strong. Your fortune will take off. It's not that you lack independence—it's that your chart's maximum energy is released through "going with the current."

3. Transformation Chart (Hua Qi Ge): You Thought You Were Jia Wood—But You've Transformed Into Earth

The Transformation chart is the most misunderstood structure. Simply put: two Heavenly Stems meet and undergo a chemical reaction, becoming a completely different element. For example, Jia Wood and Ji Earth meet and can transform into Earth—you thought you needed Water (for Wood), but you've become Earth and need completely different things.

People with Transformation charts often experience: "I thought I was this kind of person—turns out I had myself completely wrong." Transformation-chart workers may spend years doing what they think they're good at, feeling perpetually off. Until one day they accidentally stumble into a different field—and suddenly everything clicks.

How Ordinary People Turn It Around: Understand Your Chart's "Game Rules"

You might be thinking: "I don't have a special chart. I'm just a regular Balancing chart, and my BaZi is mediocre at best. Do I even have a chance?"

Yes. Plenty of chances.

The vast majority of people have ordinary charts—Five Elements not fully balanced, but not extreme enough to form a special structure. The core strategy for ordinary charts: find your Useful God. Wait for your Useful God Luck Cycle.

BaZi moves in ten-year Luck Cycles. Out of ten cycles, two or three will favor you. An ordinary person, encountering their Useful God cycle two or three times in a lifetime—each lasting ten years—gets twenty years of favorable luck. That's enough to turn everything around.

The key: before the good luck arrives—are you ready?

What to Do in Each "Destiny Phase"

🛤️ Life Fortune Roadmap

  • Unfavorable God Cycle (trough): Learn. Accumulate. Save money. Get fit. Build trust. This is the "seed-planting" phase—don't expect flowers yet. Every great tree spent years silently rooting underground before breaking through the soil.
  • Neutral Cycle (transition): Explore. Experiment. Small bets. Start a side project. Learn a new skill. Try a new city. No big decisions, but small experiments are allowed.
  • Useful God Cycle (breakout): Go all in. No hesitation, no conservatism. Release all the energy you've been accumulating. During this phase, everything you do yields twice the results with half the effort.

Truths That Reframe "Bad Luck"

  • Most people you think have "good luck" simply did the right thing at the right time. They're lucky, not superior. You just haven't hit your wave yet.
  • "Bad" charts often produce geniuses. So many historic figures had biased charts—it was the imbalance that forced creativity.
  • The "good fortune" you see has been filtered. Social media lives are always three times better than reality. Others are posting curated highlight reels; you're living the raw footage.
  • Late bloomer charts are more stable than early bloomer charts. The person who went viral at 20 may vanish at 30. The person who starts at 35 walks a gentler slope—but rises steadily.
  • Your current pain might just be "Luck Cycle transition" detox symptoms. The period right before and after a Luck Cycle shift is violently turbulent—you think you're falling, but you're actually just turning a corner.

If You Only Remember One Thing

The greatest value of destiny analysis isn't telling you whether you'll get rich or get promoted—it's telling you: where you are right now in the journey.

If you feel "my fate is bad"—it might just be because you're in an Unfavorable God cycle or a Luck Cycle transition, and you don't know it. You don't know your chart structure, what strategy to use, which Luck Cycle to wait for. You're like a player thrown onto the field without knowing the rules—of course you'll feel incompetent.

But once you know the rules—your chart structure, when your Luck Cycles come, what to do and what not to do—you'll realize: your fate isn't bad. You just haven't stepped onto the field yet.

Brother Fang says: I've seen too many people experience their biggest life transformation between ages 28 and 32. Not because they changed—because their Luck Cycle changed. Before the shift, they felt like a failure. After the shift, they discovered they were a treasure all along. What you're waiting for is simply that step.
