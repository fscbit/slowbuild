Tarot Beginner's Guide: The Three-Card Spread (Past · Present · Future)

June 21, 2026 · Tarot Tutorial

Part 1: Tarot Fundamentals

A standard Tarot deck has 78 cards, divided into two parts: the Major Arcana (22 cards) and the Minor Arcana (56 cards). The Major Arcana depict life's major lessons and the spiritual journey — from The Fool to The World, forming a complete path of soul evolution. The Minor Arcana is divided into four suits — Wands, Cups, Swords, and Pentacles — each with 14 cards, corresponding to action, emotion, thought, and the material world respectively.

For beginners, the three-card spread is the easiest to pick up yet also one of the most insightful spreads available.

Part 2: The Meaning of the Three-Card Spread

The three cards, from left to right, represent:

  • First Card (Left) — Past Influence: Events that have already occurred or fading energies that shaped your current situation.
  • Second Card (Center) — Present State: The core issue you're facing now or the current energy field. This is the heart of the reading.
  • Third Card (Right) — Future Trend: The likely outcome if things continue on their current trajectory. Note that this is not a fixed destiny, but a directional hint.

The beauty of the three-card spread is that it doesn't treat cards in isolation. Through the "Past → Present → Future" narrative arc, it weaves a complete story. For example, if the first card is the Three of Swords (heartbreak), the second is The Hermit (introspection), and the third is The Star (hope) — you have a complete narrative from pain through reflection to healing.

Part 4: How to Ask Questions for Accurate Answers

Tarot prefers open-ended questions rather than simple yes/no questions. Here are some examples of good vs. bad questions:

  • ❌ "Will they be with me?" → ✅ "What direction is my relationship with this person heading?"
  • ❌ "Should I quit my job?" → ✅ "What would happen if I accepted a new job opportunity?"
  • ❌ "Will I get rich this year?" → ✅ "How can I improve my current financial situation?"

The key is to be specific, focused, and open-ended. The more specific your question, the more targeted the guidance Tarot can provide.

Part 5: Sample Reading Walkthrough

Let's say you ask: "What direction should my career take right now?"

You draw three cards:

  • Past — Eight of Cups (Reversed): You once felt the urge to leave your current situation and seek new horizons, but fear of the unknown held you back. The reversed Eight of Cups suggests you've been circling in place.
  • Present — Ace of Wands: Right now is a moment bursting with new opportunities! The Ace of Wands represents new beginnings, creative spark, and the impulse to act. Time to take that first step.
  • Future — Nine of Pentacles: If you seize this opportunity and put in the work, the future holds tangible rewards and independent success. The Nine of Pentacles is a card of self-achievement.

Overall interpretation: Your past hesitation was only human. But the opportunity is right in front of you now. If you dare to act, the future holds rich rewards.
