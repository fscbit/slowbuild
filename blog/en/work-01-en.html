Working Late & Still Getting Scolded? Use BaZi to Find Out When Your Career Slump Will End

June 21, 2026 · 6 min read

It's 11:30 PM. You finally close your laptop. The last subway train left ten minutes ago. You open your ride-hailing app—47 people ahead of you in the queue. You open WeChat. Your manager's latest message in the work group: "Tomorrow's morning meeting is moved to 8 AM. Revise the proposal one more time." You stare at the message, your thumb hovering over the screen, not sure whether to reply "Got it" or just turn off the phone.

Sound familiar? When work goes wrong, it feels like the entire universe is conspiring against you—you revise the proposal eight times and your boss still isn't satisfied, colleagues dump their mess on your desk, you've been working your tail off but your performance metrics tank, and your payslip makes you want to cry. You start to wonder: Am I just not cut out for this working life?

I'm not here to serve you motivational quotes. I want to talk about—from the perspective of BaZi destiny analysis—why some periods feel like you can't do anything right, and most importantly—when this slump will finally end.

BaZi Career Analysis: What You're Naturally Suited For

Before you say "I don't believe in this stuff"—treat what I'm about to say as a reference framework. Its biggest value isn't prediction; it's helping you understand why certain things feel especially draining for you.

In BaZi career analysis, the most core concept is called "Useful God" (Yong Shen). In plain language: it's the element your destiny most needs, or the element that makes you feel most at ease.

🔍 Quick Self-Check: What's Your Career Useful God?

  • Favors Wood: Suited for work requiring creativity, growth, expansion — teacher, copywriter, designer, sales/business development
  • Favors Fire: Suited for work requiring enthusiasm and expressiveness — public speaking, performance, marketing, content creation
  • Favors Earth: Suited for work requiring stability and accumulation — accounting, administration, engineering, real estate
  • Favors Metal: Suited for work requiring decisiveness and precision — management, law, finance, technical specialist
  • Favors Water: Suited for work requiring flow and communication — trade, logistics, consulting, interpersonal coordination

Here's an example: someone who favors Wood gets stuck in a cubicle doing repetitive Excel reports every day. They might genuinely struggle. Not because they lack ability—it's an energy mismatch. It's like trying to plant a tree in concrete—it's not that the tree is defective, it's that the ground is wrong.

So if your work has always felt like an uphill battle, step one isn't doubting your capabilities—it's checking whether you're doing something that goes against your nature.

Annual Clashes: Why This Year Especially Sucks

Many people have this experience: two years ago you were thriving at work, everything you touched turned to gold; this year, suddenly nothing works, everything falls apart. Your abilities haven't regressed—the annual energy is messing with you.

The annual cycle (Liu Nian) is the energy field of a given year. In BaZi, the year you were born contains your "Year Pillar"—it represents your foundation in the broader environment. When the energy of a particular year clashes with the energy of your own foundation, it's called a "clash" or "control."

The classic workplace struggle signal: When the annual Heavenly Stem or Earthly Branch clashes with your "Officer Star" (the element governing career), your work experience becomes—difficult bosses, promotion dead-ends, failed bids, abandoned projects, and obstacles at every turn.

Analogy: your nature is a tree (Wood element), and this year is a Metal year. Metal chops Wood. Of course you feel terrible—like an axe is hitting you. But it's not your problem; this year's larger energy field is simply at odds with your natural attributes.

2026 is Bing Wu year (Bing is Fire, Wu is also Fire)—extreme Fire energy. If your BaZi already has lots of Fire, this year adds fuel to the flames, making you impulsive and prone to clashes with authority. If your BaZi is weak in Water, the Fire-Water clash will leave you feeling drained and depleted. If everything feels wrong lately, before you blame yourself, check if the annual cycle is the real culprit.

The Officer Star and Resource Star: Your Two Key Career Energy Points

In BaZi, the two things most relevant to your career are the "Officer Star" and "Resource Star." No need to memorize terminology—just know this:

Officer Star represents your superiors, rules and systems, and your position within the organizational structure. If the Officer Star is favorable to you, you'll naturally earn your boss's recognition. If it's unfavorable, you'll constantly feel suffocated by the system.

Resource Star represents your backing, mentors, and support network. People with a strong Resource Star always have someone watching their back at work. When the Resource Star is damaged, you'll feel isolated—when things go wrong, nobody speaks up for you.

If you're currently in a year where your "Officer Star is being clashed" or "Resource Star is being controlled"—it truly isn't about your competence. You're going through a destined "career Mercury retrograde."

Finding Your Turning Point: Three Things You Can Start Doing Now

Step 1: Understand What This Year Means for You

You don't need to learn BaZi. Just generate your chart. Once generated, look at three key things: what element is your Day Stem, what element is this year's Year Stem, and what's the relationship between them. If your Day Stem is controlled by the Year Stem, this is a "pressure year." If your Day Stem generates the Year Stem, this is a "depletion year." Once you know this, at least you'll realize: it's not all my fault—it's the year's influence.

Step 2: Adjust Your Work Rhythm Using Your Useful God

Once you know your Useful God, there are lots of small things you can do to adjust your state. For example, if you favor Wood: put a green plant on your desk, wear green, walk under trees during breaks. If you favor Fire: eat more red foods, get more sunlight, don't get stuck in windowless conference rooms. These seemingly esoteric actions are actually aligning yourself with your natural energy frequency.

Step 3: Wait for the Wind to Shift

The core wisdom of destiny analysis is this: luck runs in cycles. It won't always be good, and it won't always be bad. Being in a trough right now only means one thing—the uphill is coming. BaZi operates on "ten-year Luck Cycles" (Da Yun)—every decade brings a new wave of energy. If you're in the transition period between two Luck Cycles (about two years around the shift), turbulence and dissatisfaction are actually normal, because your entire energy system is recalibrating.

How to spot a Luck Cycle transition: If in the last year or two you suddenly feel "everything is different from before"—things that used to come easily now feel impossible, things you used to hate now suddenly interest you—congratulations, you're likely transitioning between Luck Cycles. This isn't bad; your life is simply turning the page.

Workplace Survival Guide: Strategies for the Slump

With all that said, what can you actually do?

  • Don't quit during a slump (unless you already have another offer). Decisions made during bad luck periods tend to be jumping from one frying pan into another fire.
  • Don't go head-to-head with your boss. It might not be personal—annual energy is making you two clash. Keep your distance and minimize direct conflict.
  • Treat this period as "training arc." When luck is bad, build skills, save money, get fit. When luck turns, you'll be ready.
  • Try a different seat. In BaZi-based feng shui, directions carry elemental energy. If you favor Wood, sit on the east side of the office; favor Metal—west; favor Fire—south. Sometimes just moving your workstation changes everything.

One honest closing thought: the working life is inherently tough. What destiny analysis gives you is an instruction manual—telling you when to charge ahead, when to hold your ground, and when to switch lanes. It's not about accepting fate; it's about using the path of least resistance to get through the hardest times.
