Will Your Boss Go Crazy Next Week? Tarot Three-Card Weekly Fortune Spread for Workers

June 21, 2026 · 7 min read

Sunday night, 9 PM. You're lying in bed in your rental, scrolling your phone. A message from your boss in the work group: "All-hands meeting Monday morning. Important changes to announce." Your heart instantly clenches—important changes? Layoffs? Restructuring? New management?

You want to know what next week holds, but you have neither psychic intuition nor clairvoyance. Your only "prediction tool" is your zodiac app's daily horoscope saying "This week you need to be patient"—no kidding, every week requires patience.

Today, I'm teaching you something more specific and interactive: Tarot three-card spread for weekly fortune. You don't need to be a Tarot master. You don't need to memorize 78 card meanings. As long as you have a Tarot deck (or a Tarot app on your phone), spend five minutes drawing one card each for your work, love, and finances next week.

What Exactly Is Tarot? Don't Let the "Mysticism" Label Scare You

At its core, Tarot is a "projection tool." The card you draw isn't determined by some mystical force—it's your subconscious, through random selection, picking an image that reflects your current state and future trends. The symbols, figures, and colors on the card trigger associations and insights—these associations and insights are Tarot's real value.

Analogy: you go to a fortune teller and ask "Should I quit my job?" The teller says "Draw a card." You draw The Fool—representing new journeys, adventure, acting without considering consequences. Seeing this card, an answer immediately forms: either you think "Yes, I should be braver," or "No, I can't ignore the consequences." Either way—the answer was already in your heart. Tarot just helps you say it out loud.

A standard Tarot deck has 78 cards: 22 Major Arcana and 56 Minor Arcana. As a beginner, you only need to know the 22 Major Arcana—they're the "main characters" of Tarot, each with clear meaning.

The Three-Card Spread: A Worker's "Weekly Fortune Scanner"

The three-card spread is the most classic, simplest Tarot spread. Three cards, left to right:

  • Card 1 (Left): Work / Career — Your main energy in the workplace next week
  • Card 2 (Center): Love / Relationships — How you'll interact with people around you next week
  • Card 3 (Right): Finances / Material — Your wallet status next week

Steps:

  1. Find a quiet spot. Take three deep breaths.
  2. Form a question (e.g., "What should I watch out for next week?").
  3. Shuffle, cut, and intuitively draw three cards. Reveal left to right.
  4. Check the quick reference below for basic meanings, then interpret in your own context.

Worker's Edition: 22 Major Arcana Quick Reference (Focus on These)

  • 20 Judgment: Reckoning, review. Work—past efforts or slacking will be visible this week. Finances—possible refund of previously deducted money.
  • 21 The World: Completion, fulfillment. Work—a phase ends; you'll feel satisfied. Finances—good milestone if you can save.

Live Demo: Reading a Worker's Weekly Spread

Suppose this week you draw:

  • Work position: The Hanged Man
  • Love position: The Magician
  • Finances position: The Devil

Interpretation:

The Hanged Man in the work position—this week your work may stall. Projects shelved, boss away on business ignoring you, proposals disappearing into the void. Don't panic. The Hanged Man tells you to "shift perspective"—maybe things aren't stuck; maybe you've been using the same approach on the same problem. Try a different angle.

The Magician in the love/relationships position—this week your interpersonal interactions will flow smoothly. You can handle difficult colleagues, persuade stubborn leadership. The Magician means you hold the cards. Your communication skills and expertise are on point; others will be drawn in by your energy. Use this to initiate conversations you've been meaning to start.

The Devil in the finances position—RED ALERT. This week you'll feel intense shopping urges or speculative impulses. "This bag is on sale!" "This crypto is about to moon!"—The Devil is the voice in your ear whispering "Buy it, buy it." This is not a joke: when The Devil is in your finance position, make zero investment decisions. Buy nothing that seems "too good a deal."

Practical Tips: Using Tarot in Real Life

  • Draw once every Sunday night to set the tone for the week. Review again Monday morning to reinforce mental preparation.
  • Don't be superstitious. Tarot is "reference," not "verdict." Drawing Death doesn't mean you'll die. Drawing The Sun doesn't mean you'll win the lottery. It's a thinking framework—helping you consider problems more comprehensively.
  • Ignore reversals for now. As a beginner, upright meanings are enough. Reversals are the "blocked version" or "inner version" of the energy. Add them once you're comfortable.
  • No physical deck? Use an app (Labyrinthos, Galaxy Tarot recommended) or SlowBuild's online Tarot tool.
  • Record your draws. Note them weekly. Review on weekends to see accuracy. Over time you'll notice—accuracy beats inaccuracy, and it's more accurate when you're in a balanced state. Because Tarot connects to your own intuition.
Brother Fang says: Tarot's greatest use isn't predicting the future—it's giving you a weekly ritual of "stopping to examine yourself." In an era where everyone is chased by deadlines, carving out five minutes to look at what your next week might bring—the act itself already reduces anxiety.
