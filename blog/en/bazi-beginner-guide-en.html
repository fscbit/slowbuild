BaZi Beginner's Guide: Read Your Destiny Chart at a Glance

June 21, 2026 · BaZi Basics

Part 1: What Is BaZi?

BaZi, formally called the "Four Pillars and Eight Characters," is derived from a person's year, month, day, and hour of birth, expressed using Heavenly Stems and Earthly Branches. The Year Pillar, Month Pillar, Day Pillar, and Hour Pillar each contain two characters (one Stem + one Branch), totaling eight characters — hence the name "BaZi" (literally "Eight Characters").

The origins of BaZi trace back to the Han Dynasty, maturing through the Tang and Song periods. It is the core tool of traditional Chinese destiny analysis. Its theoretical foundation is Yin-Yang and the Five Elements — by mapping the Five Element attributes of each Stem and Branch, and analyzing the relationships of generation (sheng) and control (ke) among the elements, one can infer a person's life trajectory.

Part 3: How to Construct Your Four Pillars

1. Year Pillar

The dividing line is Li Chun (Start of Spring), NOT Chinese New Year! For example, Li Chun 2026 falls on February 4. Anyone born before that date belongs to 2025 (Yi Si year); after it belongs to 2026 (Bing Wu year). 2026 is Bing Wu, so the Year Pillar is "Bing Wu."

2. Month Pillar

The Month Pillar is derived from the Year Stem using the "Five Tigers Chasing the Month" method. In simple terms: if the Year Stem is Jia or Ji → Month 1 starts with Bing Yin; Yi or Geng → starts with Wu Yin; Bing or Xin → starts with Geng Yin; Ding or Ren → starts with Ren Yin; Wu or Gui → starts with Jia Yin.

3. Day Pillar

The Day Pillar is the hardest to calculate manually. Most people use a Chinese calendar (Wan Nian Li) or an online tool. The Day Stem is called the "Day Master" or "Self Element" — it represents you and is the core of all BaZi analysis.

4. Hour Pillar

The Hour Pillar uses the "Five Rats Chasing the Hour" method, derived from the Day Stem. In simple terms: based on the Day Stem, start counting from the Zi (Rat) hour and push forward.

Part 4: The Five Elements — Sheng and Ke Cycles

The Five Elements are the core theory of BaZi analysis. Their relationships fall into generation (Sheng) and control (Ke):

  • Generating Cycle (support): Wood → Fire → Earth → Metal → Water → Wood
  • Controlling Cycle (restraint): Wood → Earth → Water → Fire → Metal → Wood

In BaZi, having too much or too little of any element affects the chart's balance. For example, if your Day Master is Wood and your chart has abundant Water, that's strong "Resource" energy (Water nourishes Wood — lots of support). If Metal is abundant, that's strong "Officer" energy (Metal chops Wood — lots of pressure).

Part 6: How to Tell What Element You're Missing

A lot of people obsess over "what element am I missing?" — but the better question is "is my Five Element distribution balanced?" Here's the process:

  1. Construct your eight characters (Four Pillars)
  2. Map each Stem and Branch to its Five Element
  3. Count the frequency of each element
  4. Analyze strength and weakness — don't blindly add what's missing! If your Day Master is weak, what you need most is the element that generates you (Resource) and the element that is the same as you (Peer), not necessarily the element that's missing.

Part 7: Understanding Luck Cycles and Annual Influences

Luck Cycles (Da Yun): These change every 10 years and determine your life's major trends. Males born in Yang years (Jia, Bing, Wu, Geng, Ren) and females born in Yin years go forward; vice versa goes backward. The starting age calculation is complex — just use an online tool.

Annual Influence (Liu Nian): The Stem and Branch of the current year. The interaction between the annual energy and your BaZi determines your specific fortune for that year.

Part 8: A Real Example

Let's use a male born May 20, 1990 at noon:

Year Pillar: 1990 is Geng Wu year → Geng Wu

Month Pillar: Geng year starts Month 1 with Wu Yin. May 20 is after Li Xia, Si month → Xin Si

Day Pillar: Check Chinese calendar → Yi You

Hour Pillar: Day Stem Yi, Yi-Geng day starts from Bing Zi, noon is the 7th period → Ren Wu

Full BaZi: Geng Wu / Xin Si / Yi You / Ren Wu (Day Master: Yi Wood)

Quick analysis: Day Master Yi Wood born in Si month (peak Fire season) — Wood is not strong. But Heaven Stems have Ren Water (Direct Resource) and Geng-Xin Metal (Officers). Earthly Branches have Wu Fire (Output) and You Metal (7 Killings). Overall, the Day Master is on the weak side and benefits most from Water (Resource) and Wood (Peer) for support.

Part 9: Common Misconceptions

  • ❌ Just add whatever element is missing: Not necessarily! If a certain element is overabundant in your chart, missing a small amount of it might be fine — or even beneficial. The key is analyzing your Day Master's strength and overall balance.
  • ❌ More elements = better chart: Balance is what matters. A chart with evenly distributed Five Elements, where generation and control are harmonious, is often better than one with an extreme standout element.
  • ❌ BaZi determines everything: BaZi reveals your innate destiny blueprint. Postnatal effort, environment, and choices matter just as much. Two people with the same BaZi can have completely different lives.
  • ❌ A "bad" BaZi means a bad life: BaZi has characteristics, not absolute judgments of good or bad. Understanding your BaZi helps you know yourself better — amplify strengths, mitigate weaknesses.
