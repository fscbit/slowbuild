Boss Picking on You, Coworkers Dumping Blame? BaZi Ten Gods Reveal Your Workplace Relationship Blueprint

June 21, 2026 · 7 min read

You're at your desk, staring at a WeChat message for three seconds. Your boss wrote: "You should reflect on this issue." The problem? You have no idea what "issue" he's talking about. You glance at the coworker to your left—the same one who, in last week's meeting, described your proposal as "something our team came up with together"—typing intently. From the screen reflection, it looks like... online shopping.

You take a deep breath. You reply: "Got it, I'll optimize." Then you quietly open Word and reread your resignation letter template for the hundredth time.

Workplace relationships are the #1 source of mental overhead for working people. The work isn't the hard part—it's the coworkers that drain you. The salary isn't low—but your blood pressure is high every day. The worst part: you feel like you haven't done anything wrong, yet someone is clearly targeting you.

Today, using BaZi's "Ten Gods" system, let's thoroughly unpack: Why does your boss keep picking on you? Why do colleagues throw you under the bus? And most importantly—how to fix it.

BaZi Ten Gods: Your "Workplace Relationship Manual"

Once your BaZi is generated, beyond the Five Elements, there's a set of "Ten Gods" labels. The Ten Gods compare your Day Master (representing you) against the other seven characters, producing ten relationship types. For the workplace, focus on these:

👥 Workplace Relationship Quick Reference: Ten Gods Map

  • Officer / 7 Killings → Your boss, superiors, system pressure
  • Resource Star → Your backing, mentors, support network
  • Peer / Rob Wealth → Your colleagues, competitors, peers
  • Output / Hurting Officer → Your talent, expression, subordinates or newcomers
  • Wealth Star → Resources and projects you may manage

Just remember the three core pairs: Officer/7 Killings = boss, Peer = colleagues, Resource = supporters. Let's unpack each.

Officer & 7 Killings: Your Relationship Pattern with Authority

Officer energy splits into two types: Direct Officer and 7 Killings.

Direct Officer is principled restraint. If Direct Officer is favorable in your BaZi, your boss is generally fair, rule-abiding. They manage you because it's their job—not personal. Your dynamic is "professional and by the book." Do your work, they won't hassle you.

7 Killings is far less polite. It represents "irrational pressure." If 7 Killings is strong and unfavorable in your BaZi, your boss is likely a headache—passive-aggressive, emotional, nitpicking without reason. Everything you do is wrong, and not doing anything is also wrong. It's not your fault—your boss has simply been cast as your "7 Killings" role in this life script.

Key distinction: Are you being managed or crushed?
Check your chart: If Officer/7 Killings is your Useful God—your boss's discipline is training you, helping you grow. If it's your Unfavorable God—their management is draining and targeting you. Same external behavior, different meaning: the former is worth enduring, the latter warrants leaving sooner rather than later.

How to handle a "7 Killings" boss?

7 Killings fears the Resource Star. Resource means "someone has your back." Practical advice: find someone with influence in the organization who's in your corner. A senior colleague, a leader from another department, even HR. When your 7 Killings boss wants to target you, what they fear isn't you—it's damaging their relationship with your protector.

Another method: use Output to subdue 7 Killings. Output is your professional ability and deliverable quality. If your work is good enough and you've made yourself hard to replace, even a 7 Killings boss won't mess with you casually. Bottom line: competence is the best shield.

Peer (Bi Jie): Your Relationship Pattern with Colleagues

Peer represents people at your level—colleagues, classmates, same-age competitors. If Peer is your Useful God, your coworker relationships tend to be good—mutual help, shared burdens. But if it's your Unfavorable God—congrats, you're that person with "terrible colleague luck."

The classic Peer-as-unfavorable experience: colleagues steal credit, dump blame, badmouth you behind your back. You help them ten times, they don't help you once, and they think that's normal. You teach them everything, they learn it, then tell the boss they figured it out themselves.

Why? Because Peer's essence is "someone just like you, coming to take your stuff." In destiny terms, Peer robs your "Wealth" and "Officer"—your salary, bonuses, promotion opportunities.

How to handle Peer-type colleagues?

  • Keep distance. Don't treat coworkers as best friends. Peer-as-unfavorable types are most likely to get "stabbed after kindness." Keep it professional.
  • Document your contributions. Email trails, documents, clear attribution in weekly reports. Make sure leadership knows what you did.
  • Reduce shared-interest situations. Peer robs wealth hardest when interests overlap—same bonus pool, same promotion slot. Avoid directly competing with these people for the same thing.
  • Strengthen Resource Star. Resource dissolves Peer—find someone above your level to speak for you. Peer dares not act up in front of Resource.

Who's Most Prone to Attracting Workplace Snakes?

In destiny terms, those with strong, unchecked Hurting Officer (Shang Guan) attract the most schemers. Hurting Officer represents talent, edge, rebelliousness. Too much makes you immensely capable but visibly sharp—snakes naturally swarm around bright lights. Not because you're wrong, but because you're too brilliant.

Additionally, those with mixed Peer and Rob Wealth also encounter snakes frequently. Both appear strong in the chart, and the people around you will be a mix of fair-weather friends and hidden enemies.

If you're in these categories:

  • Hurting Officer type: Learn to "hide." A tree that stands tallest in the forest is the first to be felled by the wind. You have talent—you don't need to display it every time. Hold back a little.
  • Peer type: Shrink your social circle. Your energy is limited. A few close friends > a hundred acquaintances.

The Hardest-Hitting Workplace Survival Rules

Beyond destiny analysis, here are principles that work for everyone regardless of BaZi:

  • Never vent casually in the office. You think someone's listening to your rant—they might be screenshotting it to send to the boss. There's no "casual chat" at work. Every word can be forwarded.
  • Building a good relationship with your boss isn't "sucking up"—it's "reducing information asymmetry." Regularly update your boss on what you're doing and what problems you're solving. The root of a boss's distrust is always "I don't know what you actually do."
  • Snakes don't target you because they hate you—they target you because you're in their way. If someone suddenly starts undermining you, think: have you recently done something that made them feel threatened?
  • Before your competence is undeniable, staying low-key is your best camouflage. Especially in large companies or government roles.
  • If you've tried everything and it's still miserable—change the environment. Destiny-wise, some BaZi combinations are simply incompatible with certain types of organizations. You're not unsuited to work—you're unsuited to this environment.

Workplace dynamics sometimes aren't about right or wrong. If you have heavy Officer energy in your chart, you'll naturally attract strict bosses. If you have heavy Peer energy, competitors will always orbit you. It's not your fault. But you have a choice—you can see your pattern clearly and respond with smarter strategies.
